\section{Separating Hyperplanes, Lagrange Multipliers, KKT conditions and Convex Duality}

We first introduce separating hyperplanes and do some stuff we saw in functional analysis, that for two closed, convex, disjoint sets a separating hyperplane always exists.

Then we introduce lagrange multipliers to convex optimization problem

\begin{align*}
    \min _y \mathcal{E}(y) \\
    \textbf{with} \mathbf{Ay = b} \\
    c(\mathbf{y}) \leq 0
\end{align*}

We introduce the \textit{Lagrangian}

\begin{align*}
    \mathbf{L(y,x,s) = \mathcal{E}(y) + x^\top (b-Ay) + s^\top c(y)}
\end{align*}

And we deal with the problem

\begin{align*}
    \max_{x,s, s\geq0} L(x,s)
\end{align*}

Where $L(x,s) = \min_y L(y,x,s)$.

We show something called \textit{weak duality}, that is $L(y,x,s) \leq \mathcal{E} (y)$ and that assuming some conditions (\textit{Slatner's condition}) the values of both the primal and dual are equal, so this is \textit{strong duality}.

\subsection{Sparating Hyperplane Theorem}

Let $A, B \subset \R ^n$ be two convex and discjoint sets $(A \cap B = \emptyset)$. We can show that there always exists a hyperplane separating those two sets, so that $A$ is on one side of the hyperplane and $B$ is on the other.

\begin{definicija}
    A \textit{hyperplane} in $\R^n$ is a set of the form $\{ x \in \R ^n, x \cdot \nu = \mu \}$ for some $\nu \in \R^n, \nu \neq 0$ and $\mu \in \R$. We say that $H$ has a normal $\nu$ and threshold $\mu$.
\end{definicija}

Hyperplane divides $\R^n$ into two halfspaces.

\begin{definicija}
    We say a hyperplane $H (\nu, \mu)$ \textit{separates two sets} $A, B$ $\iff$
    \begin{align*}
        \forall a \in A, a \cdot \nu \leq \mu \\
        \forall b \in B, b \cdot \nu \geq \mu
    \end{align*}

    If we have strict inequalities it st strictly separates.
\end{definicija}
